# HR People Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)

This project is a Power BI dashboard built to analyze employee data from 2012 to 2023.

It focuses on three main areas:
- Headcount (total employees)
- Employee retention
- Employee turnover

The dashboard helps HR understand employee distribution, retention trends, and reasons why employees leave.


## Problem
The company did not have a clear way to track employee trends.

- No visibility into total workforce across departments  
- Difficult to track retention and turnover over time  
- No clear understanding of why employees are leaving  
- Hard to compare employee data across job roles and demographics  


## Objective
- Track total employees over time  
- Analyze retention trends from 2012–2023  
- Identify departments and roles with high turnover  
- Understand employee demographics (age, gender, education)  
- Find key reasons for employee exits  


## Headcount Analysis

![Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/hr-report/raw/main/images/headcount.jpg)

**Total Employees: 3,409**

### Key Findings:
- Most employees are **individual contributors (2,551)**  
- Top departments:
  - Software (660)  
  - Sales (510)  
- **79.9% employees are onsite**, only 20.1% remote  
- Gender split:
  - Male: 60.9%  
  - Female: 39%  
- Majority employees fall in **age range 25–45**  
- Most employees have a **Bachelor’s degree (2,686)**  


## Employee Retention Analysis

![Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/hr-report/raw/main/images/retention.jpg)


**Retention Rate: 84%**  
- Starting Headcount: 409  
- Ending Headcount: 342  

### Key Findings:
- Retention improved steadily from **~92% to 100%** over time  
- **Team Leads have highest retention (~91.8%)**  
- Top departments for retention:
  - Operations (~89.2%)  
  - Administration (~88.9%)  
- Employees with higher education show **better retention trends**  

---

## Employee Turnover Analysis

![Power BI Report](https://github.com/git-data-share/hr-report/raw/main/images/turnover.jpg)

**Turnover Rate: 43%**  
- Total Employees Left: 729  

### Key Findings:
- **82.17% exits are voluntary**  
- Top reasons for leaving:
  - Found a better opportunity (179 employees)  
  - More flexible benefits (178 employees)  
  - Better salary (150 employees)  
- Highest turnover seen in:
  - Managers (~52.3%)  
  - Directors (~51.8%)  
- Departments like **R&D and Sales** show higher attrition  

## Key Insights

- Workforce is concentrated in **Software and Sales**  
- Majority of employees work onsite  
- Retention has improved significantly over time  
- Most employees leave by choice, not layoffs  
- Salary, growth, and flexibility are main reasons for attrition  


## Challenges

- High voluntary turnover  
- High attrition at manager and senior levels  
- Some departments consistently show higher exits  
- Limited remote work adoption  


## Recommendations

- Improve salary and benefits to reduce voluntary exits  
- Focus on retaining managers and key roles  
- Provide clear career growth paths  
- Introduce flexible work policies  
- Monitor high-attrition departments regularly
